## Hi, I'm Eli

I am a Mechanical Engineering student at the University of California, Riverside, interested in nanofabrication, quantum computing, and biomedical engineering.

My work explores engineering at two distinct scales:

**The Nano-Scale:** Investigating the physics of 2D chalcogenides and quantum materials to push the boundaries of next-generation computing and material stability.

**The System-Scale:** Applying computational fluid dynamics (CFD) and systems engineering to solve complex biomedical challenges, such as neurovascular treatment and assistive robotics.

Whether I am modeling oxidation kinetics in the lab or building BCI-controlled assistive devices, my goal is the same: to translate complex physical phenomena into tangible, reliable technology.

I am a **Certified SOLIDWORKS Associate (CSWA)** with a strong foundation in CFD, Python-based automation, and systems engineering methodologies. I thrive in interdisciplinary environments where I can bridge the gap between theoretical innovation and practical implementation.

### Key Focus Areas

- **Quantum Materials & Nanofabrication**: 2D van der Waals materials, oxidation kinetics modeling, material characterization
- **Biomedical Engineering**: Hemodynamic simulation, assistive robotics, brain-computer interfaces
- **Systems Engineering**: Project management, technical documentation, design optimization
- **CAD/CAE**: SOLIDWORKS, ANSYS, CFD simulation, mesh generation

Feel free to explore my [projects](/projects) and [resume](/cv) to learn more about my work!
