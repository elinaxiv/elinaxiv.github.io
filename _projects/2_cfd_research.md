**Project Role:** Undergrad Researcher, Biomedical Microdevices Laboratory  
**Timeline:** January 2026 – Present  
**Status:** Continuing Data Analysis Phase

## Overview

This research focused on analyzing hemodynamic changes in intracranial aneurysms treated with flow diverter stents. By utilizing high-fidelity CFD simulations, the project aimed to quantify how stent deployment configurations affect blood flow dynamics and clinical outcomes.

## Technical Stack

- **Simulation Software:** ANSYS Fluent, SolidWorks
- **Methodology:** Finite Volume Method (FVM) for Navier-Stokes equations
- **Analysis:** Mesh Independence Studies using Excel/Sheets

## Key Engineering Challenges

1. **Mesh Sensitivity:** Achieving computational convergence in complex, patient-specific vascular geometries. Developed iterative mesh refinement strategies to balance simulation accuracy with computational efficiency.
2. **Boundary Condition Modeling:** Translating physiological blood flow data into realistic pulsatile velocity inlet conditions to mimic real-world hemodynamics.
3. **Quantitative Validation:** Comparing simulation data against established clinical benchmarks to ensure the model's reliability for potential pre-surgical planning.
4. **Solid Modeling:** Microscale defects when deforming the stent to fit arterial geometries caused many iterations of unsuccessful meshing, resolved through rigorous checking of each mate constraint.

## Key Achievements

- Conducted high-fidelity CFD simulations and mesh independence studies for flow diverter stents
- Modeled complex vascular geometries including bifurcating structures within the Circle of Willis
- Analyzed hemodynamic performance metrics to improve clinical outcomes
- Developing quantitative basis for stent deployment optimization
