## Overview

This research project investigates the oxidation kinetics and phase transformation behaviors of ZrTe₃ (Zirconium Pentatelluride). By developing a robust mathematical framework to model these interactions, the work aims to predict material stability and performance degradation in quantum devices.

## Technical Stack

- **Modeling Frameworks:** JMAK (Johnson-Mehl-Avrami-Kolmogorov) Model, Deal-Grove Model
- **Analysis:** Kinetic Rate Equation Modeling, Curve Fitting
- **Domain:** Material Science, Solid State Physics

## Key Engineering Challenges

1. **Kinetic Modeling:** Adapting the JMAK model to describe crystallization kinetics in thin-film chalcogenides, accounting for nucleation and growth rates.
2. **Oxidation Mechanisms:** Integrating the Deal-Grove model to characterize the transition from reaction-controlled to diffusion-controlled oxidation kinetics in ZrTe₃.
3. **Data Synthesis:** Correlating theoretical kinetic predictions with experimental observation to refine current models of material degradation.

## Key Achievements

- Developed comprehensive theoretical framework for predicting lifecycle of 2D materials
- Created mathematical models for oxidation kinetics in quantum materials
- Conducted experimental characterization through nanofabrication workflows
- Performing transport-kinetics analysis on van der Waals materials and topological superconductors

## Research Contributions

- Publishing findings in peer-reviewed research papers
- Contributing to understanding of material stability in next-generation computing devices
- Advancing knowledge of 2D material degradation mechanisms
